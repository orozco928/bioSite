Biography

The fundamentals of culinary and business management were an everyday practice for Angelica from a young age. The family craft would be passed onto her from her mom Praxedes, a stoic woman whose business would not only sustain their family but secure an education. Forward progress for their humble beginnings in a declining Poza Rica, Veracruz.

She leaves home at 16, and marries soon after. The enterprising pair would go ont to grow a seafood business into a state-wide enterprise. Despite their successes, they would part ways due to a gambling addiction and financial abuse on his part. Contending with her first pregnancy, she was moving forward and away to establish herself a new beginning.

Pregnant and starting over, she heads north to opportunity in the United States. The early years were isolating, but she would quickly ingrain herself in the community working in several popular restaurants before moving to restaurant management and then starting her own food business.

She lives and breathes Mexican food, dancing with the distinctive flavors of her home region on the dishes that are already so popular here in the Southwest. Her signature tamales are a local favorite here, her current menu offering 7 different kinds.

Angelica is also proud to have raised 4 children (17 - 28 years of age) with her husband Rogelio, who passed away in May of 2020. She describes her experience as a mother with great fondness, beaming with pride at the early-life accomplishments of her children like home ownership, college graduations and the like.

She plans to continue operating her food truck for several years to come, but leaves the door open for one of her own to carry the family business when the time is right.
